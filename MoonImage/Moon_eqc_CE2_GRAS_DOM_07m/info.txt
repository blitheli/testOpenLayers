Abstract : On October 1st, 2010, Chang'E-2 (or CE2) was successfully launched. This was the second Chinese lunar exploration mission. Its main objective was to acquire 1.5m and 7m spatial resolution images at 15km and 100km orbit height. The CE2 Stereo Camera was one of the main payloads of CE2, which used the linear pushbroom scanning technique to capture two-line imagery of forward and backward views along the satellite flight direction; stereo view angles were 7.98deg and -17.2deg respectively. The CCD detector of the camera used a time delayed and integration CCD (TDI CCD), which allowed five integration grades such as 16, 32, 48, 64 and 96, three gains such as 0.7, 1.0 and 2.0. The setting of integration grade was determined by the gain setting, solar illumination condition, the average reflectance of lunar surface and other factors. The spectral range of CCD stereo camera was 450˜ 52m. The image width was 9.2km and 43km, the spatial resolution is 1.5m and 7m with the orbit altitude of 15km and 100km respectively. CE2 Stereo Camera data was used to generate DEM (Digital Elevation Model) and DOM (Digital Orthophoto Map) products for the whole lunar surface. Their ground spatial resolutions are both 7m. The internal precision is reported to be less than two pixels (<14m). Validation of the CE2 global map products was done via comparison of results with five LRRR locations and the LRO WAC DEM. In comparison with LRRR, the planimetric displacement is reported to be 23.9m˜94(stdv 57.2m) in longitude and 9.7m˜53.4m(stdv 37.6m) in latitude, height difference is -1.0m˜18.9m(stdv 10.1m). 2). In comparison with LRO WAC DEM, the average of height difference is reported to be -2.8m, stdv is 116.0m. See “A New lunar global topographic map products from Chang'E-2 Stereo Camera Image Data”, 2014, Ren et al, EPSC Abstracts Vol. 9, EPSC2014-344, European Planetary Science Congress 2014. The size of this data product download is 366 GB.
