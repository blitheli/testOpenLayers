Description:
Abstract:
The Apollo 15 DEM colorized confidence image is generated from the Apollo 15 DEM confidence data. In the confidence data, the values are derived from the precision values. For the colorized confidence, the value range from 10 to 14 was used, ranging from poor confidence (10) to very good confidence (14). This range was mapped to a color map varying from yellow to green. All other confidence values were mapped to fully transparent pixels.
Purpose:
This confidence color image provides an visual assessment of the "goodness" of the data in the Apollo 15 DEM.

Data_Quality_Information:
Attribute_Accuracy:
Attribute_Accuracy_Report:
Precision data is provided at each pixel and is available for download. A visual representation of the precision data is available in the Confidence Map.
Logical_Consistency_Report:
The Apollo 15 DEM precison maps are generated using the elevation expected data standard deviation of all the available Apollo 15 DEM files. The DEM confidence maps represent at each pixel, precision levels as described here: a confidence value of 10 represents a standard deviation larger than 100m, a value of 11 represents a standard deviation in the range 75-100m, a value of 12 represents a standard deviation in the range 50-75m, a value of 13 represents a standard deviation in the range 25-50m, and a value of 14 represents a standard deviation in the range 0-25m. The precision values of the A15 DEM zone are captured in the file, Apollo15_MetricCam_Prec_Global_1024ppd.tif.
Completeness_Report:
The accuracy and precision of the digital elevation map is lower in areas where the images where corrupted by noise in the camera path or scanning artifacts. While the effect of image noise is reduced by the our robust stereo matching algorithm some of the artifacts remain present in the final product. Other areas of low precision and accuracy include shadow regions where stereo correlation has poor quality and areas along the image boundaries.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The Apollo 15 DEM is computed from stereo pairs (adjacent images within each orbit) of the A15 mission. The process uses the bundle adjustment solution and Bayesian subpixel correlator in Ames Stereo Pipeline package to reduce the uncertainties in camera position and reduce the effect of image noise. Elevation values extracted from overlapping stereo pairs (each point on the Lunar surface is seen by more than two images) are averaged and stored as the best elevation estimate. The standard deviation of all elevation values is used as a measure of precision. The range of Apollo images used here include all nadir looking images of the Apollo 15 Metric zone: AS15-M-0072.cub-AS-15-M-0103.cub, AS-15-M-0136.cub-AS-15-M-0160.cub, AS15-M-0281.cub-AS15-M-1426.cub, 460-602, 872-1031, 1052-1159, 1562-2491.
