Description:
Abstract:
This is a preliminary South Pole mosaic of the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter Camera (LROC) Wide Angle Camera (WAC) images taken in monochrome mode. The global WAC mosaic provides a global morphology basemap with incidence angles between 60 and 80 deg, except in polar regions where the Sun is always very low on the horizon.
Purpose:
The LROC WAC polar mosaic provides an improved morphologic map of the Moon by acquiring images during different seasons to reduce extreme shadowing caused by low sun angles prevelant in the polar regions.

Data_Quality_Information:
Logical_Consistency_Report:
Because the images used in the polar mosaic were acquired in different seasons than the equatorial images used in the global image mosaic, and the lunar photometric function is not perfectly known, there is a brightness difference between the polar mosaics and the equatorial region of the global mosaic. In a future releases this discontinuity will be reduced.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
The south pole images were acquired from 8/10/10 to 9/19/10. Some gores were filled with data taken at other times. The polar images were map projected on the LOLA shape model (80 deg to 90 deg; N/S) and the GLD100 (60 deg to 80 deg N/S). In addition, the LOLA derived crossover corrected ephemeris and an improved camera pointing provide accurate positioning of each WAC image.
